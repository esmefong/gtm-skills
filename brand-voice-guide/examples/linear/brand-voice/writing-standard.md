# Writing Standard — Linear

The concrete rules. Apply after the voice rules in `voice-core.md`.

## Do
- **Words we use:** *ship, velocity, momentum, focus, surface, purpose-built, self-driving, planning,
  building.* Sample: *"…dashboards that surface what needs your attention."*
- **CTAs:** a single action verb with an arrow — *Plan →*, *Build →*, *Monitor →*. Never "Learn more."
- **Naming:** "Linear" is one word, a proper noun. Product versions use a lowercase *v* (*v2, v3*).
- **Capitalization:** sentence case for all headlines.
- **Punctuation:** periods, not exclamation marks. Fragments are allowed and encouraged.

## Avoid  *(built last)*
- **Banned words:** *unlock, leverage (v.), seamless, robust, revolutionary, game-changer,
  next-generation, supercharge,* and any adjective pile.
- **HIGH — Hype words** (*revolutionary, game-changer, supercharge*). Break the confident-understatement
  pillar. Instead: state the capability plainly and let it stand.
- **HIGH — Exclamation marks / emoji** in product copy. Off-voice entirely.
- **MEDIUM — "Learn more" or multi-word CTAs.** Instead: one action verb + arrow.
- **MEDIUM — Adjective tricolons** ("faster, smarter, more reliable"). Instead: one exact claim.
- **LOW — Greeting or question openers.** Linear opens on the statement, never on a warm-up.
