# Voice — Core (Universal) — Linear

Applies to every channel. Channel files may extend; never contradict.

## 1. Tonal pillars
- **Purpose-built, never general.** The core claim, repeated. Sample: *"Purpose-built for planning and
  building products. Designed for the AI era."*
- **Terse and declarative.** Says the thing, stops. Sample: *"Define the product direction."* /
  *"Built for the future. Available today."*
- **Momentum is the value.** The benefit is always speed, focus, less noise. Sample: *"Reduces noise
  and restores momentum to help teams ship with high velocity and focus."*
- **Engineer-credible and literal.** Names the real workflow, no metaphor tax. Sample: *"Designed for
  workflows shared by humans and agents. From drafting PRDs to pushing PRs."*
- **Confident understatement.** No hype, no exclamation, no emoji. The calm of a tool that doesn't need
  to shout. Sample: *"Made for modern software teams."*

## 2. Sentence-level rules
- Headlines are short declarative fragments, often 3-7 words.
- One idea per line. Cut every word that doesn't change meaning.
- Sentence case, always. No exclamation marks. No emoji.

## 3. Persona we are NOT
- Not a hype account. Not a cutesy/playful voice. Not a verbose enterprise-sales voice. Not a generic
  "all-in-one productivity" tool that could be any PM app.

## 4. The distinctiveness test
If a line needs an exclamation mark or a superlative to land, it isn't Linear — cut back until the
plain statement carries it.
