## Source D — Public writing philosophy (from Linear's changelog notes / brand page)

- Linear tells other startups to write changelogs about "things interesting to humans," to avoid
  including everything, and notes that "database migrations aren't interesting unless they result in
  performance gains or improve user experience."
- Brand rule: "Linear" is always one word, a proper noun. Product versions use a lowercase v
  (*v2 / v3*). Headlines are sentence case.
- The house style is famously terse: short declaratives, no exclamation marks, no emoji, no hype.

## Observed patterns (analyst notes, not brand copy)
- Headlines are short declarative fragments, often 3-7 words: "Define the product direction."
- Recurrent theme words: *ship, velocity, momentum, focus, purpose-built, surface, self-driving.*
- CTAs are single action verbs with an arrow. No "Learn more."
- Confident understatement ("Volume speaks for itself" energy); never breathless.
