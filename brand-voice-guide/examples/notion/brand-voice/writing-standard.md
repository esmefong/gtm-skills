# Writing Standard — Notion

The concrete rules. Apply after the voice rules in `voice-core.md`.

## Do
- **Words we use:** *together, everything, your team, capture, find, automate, workspace, source of
  truth.* Sample: *"Capture context, find answers, and automate tasks…"*
- **Address:** always *you / your*. The reader is a person, not a "user segment."
- **Punctuation/format:** ampersands are fine; the two-beat contrast ("A. B.") is a signature; one
  deliberate mid-sentence capital is an occasional flourish — used sparingly, never twice on a page.
- **Capitalization:** sentence case, warm and clean.

## Avoid  *(built last)*
- **Banned words:** *leverage, streamline, robust, solutions, organizations* (as a stand-in for "you").
- **HIGH — Enterprise jargon** (*leverage, streamline, solutions, organizations*). Breaks the warm,
  second-person pillar. Instead: say it the way you'd say it to a colleague.
- **HIGH — Cold third-person** ("Users can…"). Instead: "You can…"
- **MEDIUM — Feature dumps** with no reader or benefit. Instead: pair each capability with what it does
  *for you*.
- **MEDIUM — Hype superlatives** ("game-changing"). Instead: a plain-human superlative ("Perfect notes,
  every time").
- **LOW — Over-using the wink.** One deliberate flourish per page; more than that and it reads as cute.
