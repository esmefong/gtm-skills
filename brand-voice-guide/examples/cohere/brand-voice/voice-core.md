# Voice: Core (Universal): Cohere

Applies to every channel. Channel files may extend; never contradict.

## 1. Tonal pillars
- **Ownership, in second person.** The signature move: a possessive contrast where the customer owns
  and Cohere enables. Sample: *"Own your AI."* / *"Your data. Your infrastructure. Cohere keeps it
  that way."* / *"Our models. Your business."*
- **Short declaratives, often in triads.** Says it in the fewest words, three beats. Sample: *"Safe.
  Flexible. Independent."*
- **Sovereignty and trust, forward.** Control and trust are the frame, not a footnote. Sample: *"Your
  sovereign AI workplace."* / *"Why leading teams trust Cohere."*
- **Plain over jargon, but technically precise.** Simple words carrying serious ideas, with exact
  capability terms when they matter. Sample: *"High-performance models for agentic, multimodal,
  multilingual AI."*
- **Confident and calm, never breathless.** Enterprise-serious. No frontier-race bravado, no viral
  energy. Sample: *"Powering progress across industries."*

## 2. Sentence-level rules
- Short fragments. Favor the possessive contrast ("Your X. Our Y.") and the triad ("A. B. C.").
- Second person throughout: *your, you*. Never "enterprises can" when "you can" will do.
- Imperative verbs: *Own, Keep, Go.* CTAs are direct: *"Go North," "Let's go," "Contact us."*

## 3. Persona we are NOT
- Not a hype or frontier-lab voice. Not consumer or playful. Not jargon-drowned. Not breathless, and
  never in a model-race bragging match.

## 4. The distinctiveness test
If a line doesn't put the customer's ownership and control at the center, it isn't Cohere. Rewrite
until "your" is the point.
