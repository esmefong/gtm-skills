# Question bank

Questions for filling what the harvest can't answer (Phase 3), and for **interview mode** when the
brand has little published content to harvest. Each block maps to one directory file. Ask only what the
samples didn't already give you.

Two rules hold throughout:
- **Capture the answer verbatim.** The exact phrase is the asset; don't summarize it.
- **Push once on the important answers.** The correction ("actually, that word's not quite right") is
  usually closer to the truth than the first take.

---

## For `about-company.md`
- "In one plain sentence — not the pitch — what does the brand actually do? The version you'd give a
  smart friend outside your industry."
- "What was the specific frustration or gap that made it exist?"
- "What are the 2-3 things you've built or shipped that you're proudest of? Numbers, names, outcomes."
- **"What does the brand believe that most competitors won't say out loud — the thing you'd say at
  dinner that might start an argument?"** (Usually the richest voice material.)

## For `icp.md`
*(Skip if `icp-research` already produced an ICP — use that instead.)*
- "Describe your single most important reader as a specific person, not a demographic. Their job, their
  week, what's on their mind."
- "What's happening in their work or life the moment they find you?"
- "What words do they use for their problem? Theirs, not yours — paste a real quote if you have one."
- "Who looks right on paper but is *not* your reader — who never converts or stays?"

## For `voice-core.md`
- "Paste something you've written that felt most like you. What made it feel right?"
- "Name a brand or creator in your space whose content makes you cringe. What specifically bothers you?"
- **The relationship decision:** "If the brand were a person at a dinner table, what role does it play?"
  If they stall, offer contrast pairs and ask *why* for each:
  | Pick one | vs. |
  |---|---|
  | Mentor | Peer |
  | Simplifies complexity | Sits in complexity with the reader |
  | Projects confidence | Owns the uncertainty |
  | Challenges the reader | Meets the reader where they are |
- "What do you want someone to *feel* at the end of reading anything you publish? Not think — feel."
- "When something's complex, do you simplify it, stay in it with the reader, or show the work and let
  them follow?"
- **"Write the one sentence you'd want someone to say about the brand after three months of your
  content."** Then push: "Which word in that isn't quite true — something that sounded good but doesn't
  fully fit?"

## For channel files (`voice-linkedin.md`, `voice-email.md`, `voice-x.md`, etc.)
First confirm the channel is intentional: "Is this channel published with purpose, or is it just there
/ cross-posted?" Then, per channel:
- "Who specifically is on this channel — same reader as elsewhere, or someone different? How does their
  mindset shift here?"
- "Walk me through a piece that *worked*. What did it open with, do in the middle, close with?"
- "What would make someone immediately bounce off a piece from you here?"
- "What's your actual content mix here — promotional / educational / personal — and what do you want it
  to be?"
- "What would you never do on this channel, even if it worked everywhere else?"

Channel-specific adds:
- **Email:** "List the groups on your list. For each, what should they feel on open?" / "Best-performing
  subject line, and why you think it worked?"
- **Landing pages** (one per CTA page): "The single action you want on this page?" / "Where does the
  reader arrive from, and what must they believe before they click?" / "The one thing this page must
  never say?"
- **Discord/community:** "Who's actually typing — you, a team, mods? Does the voice need to hold across
  all of them?" / "What behavior would break trust with the community?"
- **YouTube/podcast/short-form:** "Scripted or off the cuff? Does the script voice match the delivery?"
  / "What do you open with, and what would you never open with?"

## For the Avoid section of `writing-standard.md` (built last)
- Run the universal starter list (in `output-templates.md`): confirm each item as banned, or mark it a
  deliberate exception with a one-sentence reason.
- **The AI-draft-reaction:** "Here's a short, styleless AI draft on one of your topics. Mark every line
  that makes you wince, and finish the sentence: *we'd never publish this because ___.*" These are the
  most valuable, most brand-specific tells.
- "What word or phrase do you see everywhere in your industry that you refuse to use?"
