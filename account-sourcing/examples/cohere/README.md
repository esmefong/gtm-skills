# Worked example: sourcing target accounts for Cohere

> **Real companies, public data, illustrative.** This example runs the `account-sourcing` skill for
> [Cohere](https://cohere.com) using only public sources. Every listed organization is real and every
> claim carries a cited public source and date in `accounts.json`. It is a demonstration of the
> skill, not an official Cohere document, and listing an organization asserts nothing beyond the
> cited public evidence. Personas are role-level only; no individuals are named.

## What this shows

The full pipeline on one company, end to end, with the judgment visible:

- **The ICP became the query spec.** The account-level criteria from
  [`icp-research/examples/cohere/icp.md`](../../../icp-research/examples/cohere/icp.md) translated
  directly into `rubric.yaml`. This is the composition between the two skills, mechanically.
- **The suppression pass saved the run.** The sweep found that Bell and the Government of Canada,
  two of the most obvious "targets" in Canadian regulated AI, are already Cohere partners. They sit
  in the disqualified table with citations instead of embarrassing an outreach sequence.
- **The reject pile has receipts.** SoftBank (OpenAI joint venture) and BNP Paribas (group-wide
  Mistral agreement) are disqualified on their own press releases: committed elsewhere, dated.
- **The example audits itself.** The first pass put Swisscom and Telefonica at tier 1 on evidence
  from an undated vendor-marketing page; review caught that the source map classifies vendor partner
  listings as Tier C, regraded the entries, and reran the scorer. Both accounts visibly dropped and
  the regrade is documented in `account-list.md`. The standard moved the ranking, not the reverse.
- **The tiers are honest.** The tier-2 cluster at exactly 55 exists because bank and insurer
  infrastructure posture was not evidenced at Tier A/B in one sweep, and the hiring criterion fired
  for nobody (the public ATS check came back 404 for this Workday-heavy segment, shown verbatim in
  `account-list.md`). Un-earned points stay un-granted.
- **An insight fell out.** The sovereign-AI telcos (TELUS at tier 1, Swisscom, Telefonica) look like
  platform partners in the mold of Cohere's existing Bell AI Fabric deal as much as customers, so the
  ranked table carries a motion label per account. The sourcing run surfaced a partnership lane, not
  just a target list, and it names who consumes each lane (ABM, field, partner coverage) to match the
  ICP's analyst-led buying journey.
- **The surprise ring is named.** Beyond the household names, the accounts a sales team would not
  already have (ATB Financial, Interac, the pension funds, provincial governments, EU mid-tier banks)
  sit in a verify-later ring with the rationale and next check for each, rather than being forced
  onto the list without evidence.

## Files

| File | What it is |
|---|---|
| `rubric.yaml` | The ICP translated into weights, criteria, disqualifiers, and tiers |
| `accounts.json` | 16 records with per-fact evidence (value, source, date, tier) plus routing (`owner_team`, `next_action`), scored in place by the script |
| `account-list.md` | The working doc: ranked table, self-audit, reject table, verify-laters, gaps, refresh cadence |
| `briefing.html` | The executive layer, generated by `render_briefing.py`: lanes per owning team, next action per account, rejects with reasons, disclaimer rendered |

## Reproduce the scoring and the briefing

```
python3 ../../scripts/score_accounts.py accounts.json rubric.yaml --table
python3 ../../scripts/render_briefing.py accounts.json -o briefing.html --company "Cohere" --accent "#39594D"
```

The scoring is deterministic: same inputs, same ranking, every run. The evidence gathering behind
`accounts.json` came from five public web sweeps on 2026-07-21 (bank newsrooms, telecom sovereign-AI
press, insurer deployments, competitor commitments, and a Cohere partnership check for suppression).
