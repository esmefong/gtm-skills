# Output template

The exact structure for the final ICP document. Fill every section from tagged research signal. Lead
every profile with the situation. Keep customer quotes verbatim and attributed (source + date). When
the buyer is an individual rather than a team, cut the team-only sections (noted inline).

The document has three layers:

1. **Account-level ICP** — which companies (team buyers only; skip for an individual buyer).
2. **Buyer profiles** — the situations and the people, using the house structure.
3. **Operationalization** — scoring rubric, buying-committee map, How to Win, routing, validation.

Copy the headings below verbatim into the final document.

---

```markdown
# Ideal Customer Profile — [Company / Product]

[One line: who this is for and what to anchor to it. e.g. "The single source of truth for audience
definition. Anchor copy, targeting, and product decisions to these."]

**Data sources:** [list each source, its type, and its date. State the confidence level: is this
evidence-grounded or a hypothesis to validate?]

---

## Account-level ICP  *(team buyers only — cut for an individual buyer)*

The company that fits. A rep should be able to score a new account against this in 30 seconds.

**Firmographics:** industry / vertical, company size (employees and revenue band), geography, stage.
**Technographics:** current stack that signals fit, integration readiness, adjacent tools they run.
**Behavioral signals:** observable triggers that an account is in-market (recent funding, hiring in
[function], a public initiative, a vendor switch in an adjacent category).
**Disqualifiers (explicit):** "if [X], deprioritize." "If [Y], walk." "If under [Z], not a fit."

---

## Buyer profile: [Segment / situation name]  *(repeat per segment, 1-4 total)*

*Lead with the situation, not a title. Name the segment by its situation where possible.*

### Who they are
[Role / life context and the situation they're in. Behavioral and situational first; demographics as a
secondary lens, included only where they change the buying decision or the channel.]

### The trigger (why now)
[The Priority Initiative. The recurring trigger moments, ordered by frequency, most common first. This
is what makes them unable to ignore the problem now.]
- [Trigger 1] — [1-2 verbatim quotes, attributed]
- [Trigger 2] — [...]

### What they're doing before they find us
[The status quo and the alternatives they're cobbling together now. What they do instead, and why it
isn't working.]

### What success looks like for them
[Success Factors, in their words. The tangible and the emotional outcome they need to feel it was worth
it. Ordered by importance.]
- [Outcome] — [quote]

### What almost stops them
[Perceived Barriers. What nearly kills the purchase, plus the Four Forces read: the Anxiety of switching
and the Habit holding them in place. Note which barriers led to no-purchase vs. were overcome, and how.]
- [Barrier] — [quote]

### How they compare options (decision criteria)
[The attributes they actually weigh, in priority order. Where you win, where you lose, and where
perception differs from reality.]

### The buying committee  *(team buyers only — cut for an individual buyer)*
[The 5-7 roles that must say yes. For each: role, KPI, their top barrier and decision criterion, and
their objection pattern. Then the system: who champions, who can veto, who influences whom, where deals
stall.]

| Role | KPI | Top barrier | What wins them | Veto power |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Champion | | | | |
| Economic Buyer | | | | |
| Technical Buyer | | | | |
| End User | | | | |
| Blocker (procurement/legal/compliance) | | | | |

### How to speak to them
[Concrete tone and framing rules sourced from the research. What language lands, what to lead with, what
to avoid. Use their vocabulary, not yours.]

### Where to find them (journey + channels)
[The Buyer's Journey mapped to channels: where they first hear of the category, where they research, who
they trust, which touchpoints had the most influence. This is the channel strategy.]

---

## Recipient / gatekeeper persona  *(if applicable — e.g. an approver, a partner, a parent)*
[Anyone reached *through* the buyer who shapes the decision without being the buyer. How to serve them.]

---

## Who we are NOT for
[Explicit exclusions with rationale. Each entry: who, and why they're a mismatch (price, commitment,
fundamentally different need). This prevents wasted spend and diluted positioning. Note any that could
be a future product without diluting the core.]

---

## How to win

**USP — why you, why now.** [The single most compelling reason to choose this over the alternatives,
grounded in Decision Criteria. Answers both "why you over what else I'm considering?" (differentiation)
and "why act now?" (urgency, which lives in the buyer's situation, not artificial scarcity).]

**Narrative.** [The story arc the buyer sees themselves in, starting from their world and the moment
things shifted, not from the product.]

**Differentiation.** [The 1-2 dimensions where this is genuinely different. Compete there; make those
the frame of comparison.]

**Channel strategy.** [Where to show up, from the journey above. Prioritize high-intent channels where
buyers are actively researching over low-intent browsing.]

---

## Insight-to-action routing

| Research finding | Routes to |
|---|---|
| Situation triggers ("why now") | Hero copy, ad targeting, positioning |
| Success factors | Benefit copy, email nurture, success-story selection |
| Perceived barriers | FAQ, objection handling, guarantees, testimonial selection |
| Decision criteria | Differentiation, feature emphasis, comparison pages |
| Buyer's journey | Channel strategy, content distribution, partnerships |
| Segments to attract / exclude | Audience targeting, product decisions, who to intentionally exclude |

---

## Validation plan

**Hypotheses.** [Each ICP as a testable claim with a metric and threshold: "if leads from situation X
convert at 2x baseline, this ICP is validated."]

**Next validation step per gap.** [For each unknown, the specific next move: a cohort of interviews, an
ad test, a keyword check, a win/loss review.]

**Refresh cadence.** [Quarterly deep-dive, monthly pulse-check, always-on community + funnel intake.]

---

## Gaps in this document
[Honest accounting of what is validated vs. hypothesized, what data is missing, and the next step to
close each gap. This section is required. An ICP that hides its gaps is less trustworthy, not more.]
```

---

## Notes on filling it in

- **Order segments by frequency**, most common situation first.
- **Every claim should be traceable.** If it came from a quote, attribute it. If it's an inference, say
  so. If it's an assumption, move it to Gaps.
- **Keep it to 1-4 segments.** Past 4-5, no one can operationalize it.
- **Individual-buyer adaptation:** when one person buys (consumer, prosumer, lifestyle), cut the
  Account-level ICP and the buying-committee table. Keep everything else, and lean harder on the
  emotional core within "What success looks like" and "How to speak to them."
